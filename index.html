Liquid Propellants

Propellants are the materials that are combusted by the engine to produce thrust.

Bipropellants

Bipropellant liquid rocket systems consists of a fuel and an oxidizer.They are a common propellant because of their high performance,but are more complex

Monopropellants

Several propellants can be used singularly(monopropellants),as they release energy when they decompose either by getting heated or catalyzed.


There are mainly three primary propellant type:

  • Storable
  • Space Storable
  • Cryogenic

Storable propellants

  • They aare liquid at sea level of temperature and pressure.Thus,it can be stored indefinitely in tanks.
  • The drawback of this type of propellants is that,with the exception of kerosene-based propellants,they are highly toxic,reactive,corrosive and difficult to handle
  • They are mostly hypergolic(spontaneously ignite when in contact with each other in fuels and the oxidizer)
    • Hypergolic propellants are used for small thrust applications.
    • Elimination of ignition system provides thrsust on demand and pulse mode
  • Monopropellants
    1. They are storable liquid propellants which are induced to decompose to gases in presence of a catalyst,which releases heat to convert it to thrust
    2. The performance is lower than that of bipropellant systems but it is simpler which provides a high reliability
    3. One drawback is that due to its high reliability,a high standard of cleanliness is needed
    4. Examples
      • H2O2(Hydrogen peroxide)
      • N2H4(Hydrazine)
      • UDMH(Unsymmetrical Dimethylhydrazine)

Space Storable propellants

  • They are liquid in temperature of space and heir net boiling point is 230oR(Rankine)
  • They can be stored for a longer time than cryogenic propellants in space
  • They are more energetic than most of the storable propellants,but are rarely used due to their extreme toxicity,reactivity and handling difficulty.
  • It is rarely used in operational propulsion systems.
  • Examples
    • LO2/NH3
    • C2H6
    • N2F4(Tetrafluorohydrazine)

Cryogenic propellants

  • They are liquified gases at very low temperatures(30o-230oR) and are the most energetic propellants.
  • They are hard to store for a long time as venting of the tanks are needed.
  • LO2 and LH2 are the most common cryogenic propellants
    1. LH2
      • Advantages:It has a high performance and is an excellent coolant
      • Dis-advantages:They have less density,thus this will increase tank size
    2. LO2
      • Advantages:It has a high performance and Flourine is the only better oxidizer than LO2
      • Dis-advantages:It is not selective of what it uses as a fuel but it prefers hydrogen.It also oxidizes almost everything(consumes with ozidation potential)
  • Examples
    • LH2(Liquid Hydrogen)
    • LO2(Liquid Oxygen)
    • OF2(Oxygen Diflouride)
